## Design Context

### Users
This project serves readers of long-form essays, notes, interviews, and book notes, along with writers who may adopt the theme for their own personal site. The primary job is reading comfortably for extended periods, with content remaining the center of attention.

### Brand Personality
Clean, thoughtful, writer-first. The tone should feel calm, deliberate, and personal rather than corporate or app-like.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial minimalism with strong readability and restrained visual styling. Typography should feel intentional and bookish, with enough personality to support an independent writer voice. Avoid generic startup-blog styling and avoid decorative flourishes that compete with the writing.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize reading comfort over visual novelty.
2. Make hierarchy obvious through type, spacing, and rhythm.
3. Keep styling restrained, but ensure details feel deliberate rather than default.
4. Preserve a personal, literary tone instead of a product-marketing tone.
5. Use the design token system consistently so refinements stay systematic.
